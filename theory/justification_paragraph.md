This Letter derives the bond-valence rule from Yukawa-screened electrostatics, turning a ubiquitous empirical relation into a quantitatively testable statement about local electronic screening. That framing is relevant to PRL because bond valence is widely used to analyze oxide quantum materials, including perovskites and cuprates, where cation--oxygen bonding, valence balance, and local distortion underpin ferroelectric, ionic, and correlated-electron behavior. Most importantly, the theory predicts a sharp crossover between screening and anti-screening when a cation's formal charge is distributed as one valence unit per bond. This gives the fitted parameters a direct meaning as a measure of local charge-transfer response, and the same shell-scale picture is validated against DFT charge densities and yields a transferable screening descriptor for 103 cation--oxygen species.
