This Letter derives the bond-valence rule from Yukawa-screened electrostatics, turning a ubiquitous empirical relation into a quantitatively testable statement about local electronic screening. That framing is relevant to PRL because bond valence is widely used to analyze oxide quantum materials, including perovskites and cuprates, where cation--oxygen bonding, valence balance, and local distortion underpin ferroelectric, ionic, and correlated-electron behavior. Most importantly, the theory identifies a screening/anti-screening pole when a cation's formal charge matches its coordination number, so each bond carries one valence unit: a singular crossover where the effective response changes sign, marking a change in local charge-transfer behavior that requries no fitting parameters. The same shell-scale picture is validated against DFT charge densities and yields a transferable screening descriptor for 103 cation--oxygen species.
