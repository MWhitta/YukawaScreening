This Letter derives the bond-valence exponential from a screened Coulomb interaction and turns a long-used empirical fitting parameter into a physically interpretable screening quantity. The result is both broad and testable: it predicts the full $B$--$R_0$ slope law $\beta=1/\ln(z/n)$ without adjustable constants and validates the same shell-scale picture against first-principles charge densities. Because bond valence is used across solid-state chemistry, mineral physics, and materials science, the paper addresses a broad PRL audience while delivering a conceptual advance, a falsifiable scaling law, and a transferable tabulation of screening descriptors for 103 cation--oxygen species, relevant to oxide bonding, crystal validation, and discovery.
